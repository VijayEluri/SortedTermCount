Syntax for formulas is:

Formula SyntaxMeaning
some x : A | FMLA Existential quantification over x in sort A.
all x : A | FMLA Universal quantification over x in sort A.
not FMLA Negation of a formula FMLA.
FMLA1 and FMLA2 Conjunction of formulas FMLA1 and FMLA2.
FMLA1 or FMLA2 Disjunction of formulas FMLA1 and FMLA2.
x in R The atomic formula R(x).
x->y in R The atomic formula R(x, y).
x->...->z in R The atomic formula R(x, ..., z).
x = y Equality of the variables x and y.
(FMLA) Grouping of formulas.

Variables must be lower case alpha character strings.

Relations and Sort symbols must be upper case alpha character strings.

Finally, Alloy syntax does not allow functions, and our algorithm is concerned only with the Skolem signature of a given formula, not with the structure of terms contained therein. Therefore no distinction is made between variables and more complex terms at the formula level. If you wish to use function symbols in a formula, they may be used without parentheses, e.g. fxy instead of f(x, y).
